Humanity Protocol™ | Official Website

Humanity Protocol is a decentralized identity verification system that leverages biometric technology, specifically palm scans, to authenticate individuals in digital environments. By linking a user's palm scan to their digital identity on the blockchain, the protocol ensures that only real humans participate in online spaces, addressing challenges such as bots, fake accounts, and online fraud. ​
Humanity Protocol

Key Features of Humanity Protocol:

Decentralized Identity Verification: Utilizing palm recognition technology, Humanity Protocol offers a non-invasive method to establish proof of humanity. This approach provides a secure and private means for users to verify their identity without revealing personal details. ​
RootData

Sybil Resistance: The protocol is designed to be Sybil-resistant, preventing the creation of multiple fake identities by a single entity. This ensures that online platforms can maintain integrity by allowing only genuine human interactions. ​
Humanity Protocol

User Data Ownership: Humanity Protocol empowers users with complete ownership over their data and identity. By storing identity information on the blockchain, users have control over who accesses their data, enhancing privacy and security. ​

Humanity Protocol 

Integration with Web3 Applications: The protocol serves as the human layer for Web3, providing developers with unique human authentication mechanisms. This integration facilitates the creation of decentralized applications that require verified human participants. ​

Recent Developments:

Funding and Valuation: In January 2025, Humanity Protocol raised $20 million in a funding round co-led by Pantera Capital and Jump Crypto, achieving a valuation of $1.1 billion. The funds are intended to expand the platform's reach and enhance its identity verification technology. ​
Reuters

User Adoption: Within three months of its beta launch, Humanity Protocol attracted approximately 2.9 million users who signed up for a human ID, indicating significant interest and trust in the platform's approach to identity verification. ​

MES Computing

Establishment of Humanity Foundation: The Humanity Foundation was formed to invest in projects leveraging Humanity Protocol's proof of humanity. Led by industry veterans, the foundation aims to drive innovation by funding research and development in decentralized identity verification and exploring new use cases. ​

Applications and Use Cases:

Online Platforms: By integrating Humanity Protocol, online platforms can ensure that interactions are between real humans, reducing spam and fraudulent activities.​

Humanity Protocol

Financial Services: The protocol's secure identity verification can enhance KYC (Know Your Customer) processes, streamlining onboarding and compliance for financial institutions.​

Access Control: In physical spaces, Humanity Protocol can be used for secure access to buildings or events by linking permissions to a user's biometrics. ​

Conclusion:

Humanity Protocol represents a significant advancement in decentralized identity verification, addressing critical challenges in digital interactions. By ensuring that only real humans participate in online and offline spaces, it enhances trust, security, and privacy, paving the way for more authentic and secure digital experiences.​
